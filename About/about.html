What keeps projects on track

Four decisions that determine
whether a system holds up

Not just on paper, but in day-to-day operation.

01

Start with today's site conditions

Representative sampling, load variability, and operating limits come before any design overview. Assumed averages fail at the edges, and water systems often operate there.

02

Structure the vendor engagement

We align scope, connections, and responsibilities so vendors quote on the same outline, proposals are genuinely comparable, and there are no gaps at handover.

03

Define delivery checks before start-up

Commissioning tests, QA/QC, and performance ranges agreed upfront, not negotiated at completion. That keeps delivery aligned to what was agreed.

04

Keep reuse and residuals viable

Outcome-first means verifying that reuse outlets, biosolids markets, and OPEX targets are achievable under actual operating conditions, not just in the design case.
