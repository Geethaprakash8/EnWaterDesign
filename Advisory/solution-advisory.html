Overview

We keep the advisory layer practical: what changes on site, what is practical to implement, and what must be monitored to verify improvement.

How this fits within the site

From early scoping to selection to installation support

Solution Advisory is your “middle layer”: it translates constraints into a vendor-ready direction without turning into a full design report.

Typical trigger

“We have data, vendors are talking, but we don’t have clarity on scope, coordination points, or the performance level proposals should meet.”

Principle

Outcome-led

Advisory anchored to what you need to achieve on site, not abstract concepts.

Principle

Operational fit

We plan around variability, maintainability, and the “how it actually runs” layer.

Principle

System connectivity

Water, sludge, odor, heat, and controls are connected, we treat them that way.

Principle

Measurable

We define what to monitor and how to verify improvement, with site-fit KPIs.

Engagement routes

Ways to engage

Choose the lightest engagement that creates clarity quickly, then scale only if needed.

Option

Advisory Call

A focused discussion to clarify the problem, constraints, and the fastest sensible next step.

  • Define the goal and “what changes on site”
  • Identify constraints and key unknowns
  • Confirm what information matters (minimum)

Option

Applied Scoping Session

A guided working session that turns your context into a structured pathway and action list.

  • Map system interactions (water to sludge to odor to heat to controls)
  • Define a practical pathway and decision checkpoints
  • Align stakeholders on scope and expectations

Option

Embedded Support

Ongoing advisory support during selection, integration, commissioning, and performance improvement.

  • Protect coordination points and operability
  • Set and track site-fit KPIs
  • Support ramp-up and optimisation

FAQs

A few clarifications on scope, approach, and how we keep advisory practical and implementable.

What does “solution advisory” mean in practice?
It means we focus on the decisions that influence outcomes: clarifying the pathway, protecting coordination points, and setting verification requirements so delivery and operations remain aligned.
Are odor, thermal, and automation separate topics or part of the whole?
They are both. We support each as a dedicated scope when it’s the constraint, and we also treat them as linked to water and biosolids outcomes. Odor is approached source-first, thermal is treated as an enabling connection point, and automation is treated as the verification layer (KPIs and controls).
How do you work with in-house teams and solution providers?
We work alongside your team and any solution providers you engage. The advisory role is to make the scope, coordination points, and verification logic clear, so procurement and delivery are smoother and expectations stay aligned.
What information helps you move fast?
A short description of the outcome you need, current constraints, and any known drivers. If some data is missing, we can still progress, we’ll define the minimum data set required to confirm the best next step.
Do you coordinate testing and specialist inputs?
Yes. We can coordinate and arrange testing through qualified labs and specialists (where needed), then translate results into practical decisions and monitoring logic.
What’s the simplest way to start?
Send an enquiry with your goal, constraints, and timing. We’ll recommend the most suitable entry point (call, scoping session, or embedded support) based on what creates clarity fastest.
