How We Design Plant Assets

Our work follows the plant stage by stage, defining what each stage receives, what it must achieve, and what needs to be shown before the scheme moves forward. This is how we confirm stage duties, test constraints, and show that the full scheme works coherently from inlet to final output.

What this review covers

  • Where each intervention belongs in the plant sequence: inlet, core treatment, polishing, reuse assurance, or residuals.
  • What becomes clear at each stage: space, shutdown periods, operator capacity, utility needs, and connection points.
  • How supplier scope is set with enough definition for comparable proposals, without fixing the scheme too early.

Stage 1

Inlet & source control

Influent variability, screening/primary capture, equalisation, batch logic, early odour/septicity controls.

Stage 2

Core treatment

Biological resilience (e.g., MBBR hybrids), operating ranges, operator-fit controls and verification metrics.

Stage 3

Polishing & clarification

Disc filters, PSF, coagulation/flocculation, solids guardrails and load handling.

Stage 4

Reuse assurance

Barrier treatment (UF/RO), oxidation/disinfection, monitoring and target ranges tied to end-use.

Stage 5

Residuals & odour

Dewatering/drying/compost routes, air capture & polishing, logistics and community coordination.

Stage 6

Integration & verification

Controls, connections, commissioning checks, verification logic, handover and performance review.

At each stage, the right modules are identified through a detailed review of process options, diagnostic needs, and integration requirements, so the scheme is built on considered selection, not assumption.

Batch+
What it is
Batch chemical treatment for high-precision coagulation/neutralization or selective removal.
When to apply
Intermittent/variable chemical streams; metals, phosphate, or pH control.
Where it fits
Pretreatment step or sidestream polishing.
Key design outcomes
  • Targeted removal and pH compliance
  • Precise control enabling reliable trade discharge permit compliance
  • Effective treatment of variable or intermittent streams where continuous systems would fail
Verification focus
  • Reaction pH range
  • Mixing energy and contact time
  • Sludge formation/settleability
  • Residuals routing
Integration & next steps
  • Coagulant and alkali selection confirmed against the target constituents and reaction chemistry
  • Sludge generation confirmed and dewatering route established for the batch volumes produced
  • Operator batch procedures defined to ensure consistent dosing, reaction, and safe handling
KPIs to track
  • Removal % (per target constituent)
  • Reagent kg/m³
  • Sludge volume index
  • Batch cycle time
