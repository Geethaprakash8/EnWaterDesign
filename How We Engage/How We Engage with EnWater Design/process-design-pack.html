Process Design Pack - Turning Project Need and Intent into EPC-Ready Design

One clear process design pack covering load cases, sizing, equipment schedules, connections, and review criteria - so your detailed engineering partner or EPC can move into IFC and procurement with the scope and limits already set out.

Vendor-ready Variant-based (STP+, ETP+, Retrofit...) Owner’s advisor Review-focused
What you get Still at feasibility? Start there instead

At a glance - what you get

Think of the Process Design Pack as one structured project pack. It gives you everything you need to set out the job clearly for vendors and design partners, without tying you to any one supplier or EPC partner.

Where stamped IFC is needed, we work with your engineer of record or design partners while staying on as the owner’s advisor to keep feasibility inputs, project basis, and review criteria intact through delivery.

One integrated pack

A single FlowPlan design & advisory pack that ties together process overview, design ranges, PFDs, equipment list, controls and monitoring concept, and review criteria around a clear project type.

Ready for your vendors

Structured inputs that vendors and EPCs can pick up immediately for pricing and detailing - without having to guess your intent, limits or record expectations.

Project basis & review built in

KPIs, sampling points, factory and site check points and early-operation checks are designed in from the start, so everyone knows what success looks like before equipment is ordered.

Backed by FlowPlan project variants

The same logic powers STP+, ETP+, retrofit, UFRO+ and built-environment reuse (GWTP, VWRP), so your design pack reflects project families already recognised in the market.

How this works in practice

This service usually starts once you’ve decided to procure or upgrade a plant - for example an STP+, ETP+ or onsite reuse system - and need a Process Design Pack your chosen partners can implement.

  1. Confirm the variant & objectives. We align on the FlowPlan variant (e.g. STP+, ETP+, GWTP, VWRP, UFRO+) and on the KPIs, reuse/compliance targets and constraints already agreed in feasibility or internally.
  2. Assemble the inputs. You share drawings, as-builts, current P&IDs, logs and water-quality data; where gaps exist, we specify what needs to be tested or surveyed and can coordinate this with your local lab or partner.
  3. Build the design pack in passes. We develop the process route, limits, preliminary hydraulics, electromechanical scope and controls/automation concept, reviewing each pass with your team in short working sessions.
  4. Land a complete design pack on your table. You receive a complete Process Design Pack - including a structured list of electromechanical equipment, proprietary packages and panels - with clear next steps for detailed design, procurement and commissioning.
  5. Stay on as advisor through the remaining stages. If you wish, we then support you as owner-side advisor through RFQs/specs, design reviews with your EOR/EPC, vendor alignment, factory and site checks, and early operation ranges.

What you get

FlowPlan design & advisory packs by variant

Each Process Design Pack engagement is built around a concrete project type you already recognise - an STP, an ETP, a retrofit, a membrane reuse stage, or onsite reuse such as greywater or vehicle wash water. For each variant, we assemble a combined design and advisory pack that can be delivered anywhere and then taken forward by your engineer of record, EPC or vendors. The STP+, ETP+, retrofit and ZLD-focused FlowPlan project variants are configured as vendor-ready design & advisory packs - with key project choices available to clients, leading to custom and targeted fit-for-purpose designs.

Positioning - how the Process Design Pack fits into delivery

The Process Design Pack comes between feasibility/options and detailed IFC design. We hold the process, performance and review intent; your nominated engineering partners / EPC turn this into IFC drawings and detailed construction design; your technology partners and solution providers supply and commission the equipment. The client stays in control of who does what.

Our role (EnWater Design)

We start from your FlowPlan variant and project data to configure the process route, limits, load cases, electromechanical scope and controls/automation concept. We assemble a design pack and structured equipment list that vendors can price and your partners can detail, and we stay alongside you as the owner’s advisor so design reviews, vendor proposals and commissioning all remain tied back to the agreed KPIs and review criteria.

Client’s engineering partners / EPC

Your engineering office, engineer of choice or EPC uses the design pack to develop IFC: detailed P&IDs, layouts, structural/MEP connections, I/O and cable schedules and construction sequencing. We do not replace them; we work alongside whoever you appoint so that the detailed design stays faithful to the agreed process intent.

Technology partners & solution providers

Equipment suppliers, technology partners and solution providers respond to the design pack with equipment selections, skids, panels and process guarantees. We help reconcile their proposals with the design basis, bill of electromechanical equipment and review plan. Adjustments to scope or configuration are agreed with you, and factory and site checks and on-site tests are executed by vendors and your EPC, with us available to support as project-side advisor.

How this fits with our design & delivery models

The Process Design Pack works within our broader design & delivery models. On every Process Design Pack engagement we stay in the system-planning and advisory role, while your engineering partners / EPC and technology partners take responsibility for stamped design, supply and construction. If you’d like to see how different engagement depths work in practice, you can explore our dedicated section: Our design & delivery business models .

Scope - what the pack includes

By the time you come to us for a Process Design Pack, feasibility and options have already been agreed. We treat those as fixed inputs and focus on configuring a vendor-ready process & system design pack, then staying with you through procurement, testing and early operation as owner’s advisor. The scope typically includes the building blocks below.

Confirmed design basis & limits

Feasibility outputs, options analysis and design ranges are treated as fixed inputs, not re-worked. We start from the agreed variant, limits, KPIs, hydraulics, utilities and site constraints, and use them as the starting basis for the pack.

Process route, hydraulics & load cases

End-to-end process route tying together headworks, biological/chemical stages, clarification, polishing and residuals handling. We define normal, peak and upset load cases and a clear hydraulic concept that your design partners can translate into detailed P&IDs and profiles.

Equipment integration & electromechanical scope

Core process units, packaged systems and proprietary equipment are positioned inside a defined treatment stage with clear duty points, standby logic, bypasses and connections. The outcome is a structured list of electromechanical equipment that vendors can price and your EPC can detail without guessing.

Controls, monitoring & automation concept

High-level control philosophy and monitoring intent: what is measured, how loops and interlocks behave, alarm and trip priorities, and how project basis will be logged for performance and compliance. Detailed I/O lists and panel design are then completed by your automation partners in line with this concept.

Review, testing & performance check pack

KPIs, test conditions, sampling and monitoring plan, and factory and site check points are built into the design from the start. Vendors and EPCs can see how commissioning and performance testing will be judged before equipment is ordered, reducing disputes and making guarantees concrete.

Implementation connections & owner’s-advisor support

Clear hand-off to your engineer of record, EPC and vendors: who details what, where reviews happen, and how design changes are agreed. If you wish, we stay on as owner’s advisor through RFQs, vendor alignment, design reviews, factory and site checks and early operation ranges to keep the design intent and KPIs intact.

Deliverables

The outcome is a complete Process Design Pack that can be priced, detailed and built by the teams you choose. These are the core outputs we typically land on your table.

Process design basis & sizing overview

A concise basis-of-design overview: selected route, operating range, key assumptions, and the sizing logic behind each major unit.

Design calculations + equipment schedule

Sizing sheets (tanks, hydraulics, aeration, pumps/blowers, chemical dosing, solids handling, etc.) plus an equipment schedule and datasheet basis that your EPC can carry into procurement.

PFDs, hydraulic profile & layout integration note

Process flow diagrams, indicative hydraulic profile, and a block layout / zoning note to make connections, levels, and footprint implications explicit for civil/MEP detailing.

Control overview & instrumentation outline

Control states/sequences, key loops and interlocks, setpoint ranges, alarms, and a planning-level instrumentation / I/O outline aligned to performance checks.

IFC alignment & design assurance notes (optional)

Review notes and approvals on IFC packages prepared by your EPC/detailed engineer to protect the design basis, connections, and review intent through delivery.

Procurement alignment & review records pack

Clarifications and decision notes on vendor offers (as needed), together with factory and site checks intent, commissioning checks, and performance record checkpoints that make outcomes verifiable.
