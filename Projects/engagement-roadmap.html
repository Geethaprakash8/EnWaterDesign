Pathway guide for project engagement

An effective project doesn’t move on momentum, it moves on decisions. This guide helps you identify where you are in the journey, what check is required, and what the next deliverable should be to keep procurement and delivery on-track.

Quick definitions: Project Background = feasibility, economics & risk to set the decision basis; ToR = vendor-ready performance, connections and check requirements; Vendor Selection = evaluation support and clarification rounds; Integration & Commissioning = connections, commissioning steps and handover needs; Performance Review = post-start-up verification and optimisation.

Start with decision point Set deliverables + check Confirm roles & connections

1) Pick the decision (and the check you’ll need)

A single project stage can hide very different risks. We map the decision you need to make to the basis, checks and review points required.

  • New build vs retrofit: shutdown periods, connections and operational continuity.
  • Outcome target: compliance, reuse, recovery and OPEX drivers.
  • Project basis: sampling plan, KPIs, commissioning and handover checks.

2) Identify your engagement pathway

These are sequential stages in the engagement, and most projects move through all three. The starting point depends on where the project stands now.

Define the basis
Pathway 1: Decision Basis & Feasibility

Clarify outcomes, constraints, risk position and review points before you commit, applicable across STP/ETP (incl. retrofits), VWTP/LWTP, reuse/TSE, biosolids and ZLD.

Get vendor-ready
Pathway 2: ToR, Specs & Vendor Evaluation

Translate the decision basis into a vendor-ready scope: ToR/spec pack, evaluation criteria, connections and check requirements, so proposals are comparable and easier to assess.

Deliver & verify
Pathway 3: Implementation, commissioning & optimisation

Owner-side support through installation and integration, commissioning steps, plus post-startup performance review and optimisation recommendations.

3) Confirm deliverables and connections early

We capture the inputs that decide whether the plan is ready to move: constraints, connections, responsibilities, and what should be in place at handover.

Implementation & System Build

Focus: Execute and integrate selected modules

What Happens: Coordinate with vendors, adapt layout, oversee construction

Key Tools: Vendor Integration Notes, Sequencing Plan

Deliverables: Installation & Integration Strategy

Post-Implementation Review

Focus: Validate outcomes and performance

What Happens: Review system performance and adjust where needed

Key Tools: Performance Monitoring Template, Operator Log

Deliverables: Optimization Plan

Sample STP Project

Municipal/new township STP targeting compliance with a staged path to reuse.

  • Context: Compliance risk + odour complaints; space constraints.
  • Goals: Stabilize effluent quality, reduce odour, enable reuse-ready polishing.
  • Modules (indicative): MBBR → Clarification → UF → RO; Sludge: Dewater/Dry; Odour: capture & polish.
  • Decision points: reuse class & targets, plot layout, sludge end-use.
Phased build Reuse-ready (UF/RO) Integration-first

Advisory Level & Approach

Selected pack: Feasibility & Planning (illustrative).

  • Deliverables: Options matrix, risks, phasing, vendor-ready design notes.
  • Outcome: Go/no-go and a staged pathway aligned to reuse targets.

How we work

  1. Scoping: flows, constraints, reuse/odour goals → tailored decision list.
  2. Sampling Plan: manual time-staggered composites or grabs; event playbooks.
  3. Lab checks: accredited methods; flagged results with re-test triggers.
  4. Report & Next Steps: actions, vendor-ready ToR inputs, phasing.

Sample ETP Project - Food & Bev

Facility balancing high-COD loads and hygiene-driven CIP.

  • Context: Variable loads, FOG/COD spikes from process & CIP.
  • Goals: Compliance-first, stable ops, optional reuse polishing.
  • Modules (indicative): Equalization → DAF → Aeration (MBBR/AS) → Clarification → UF → RO (if reuse); Sludge: Dewater.
  • Decision points: EQ sizing, FOG management, reuse case.
Process-aligned Compliance-first Vendor-ready ToR

Sample ETP Project - Textiles

Textile/dyeing unit managing color, salts, and variable pH.

  • Context: High color, pH swings, TDS from dye/salt use.
  • Goals: Permit compliance; brine/salt management; optional reuse.
  • Modules (indicative): Equalization → pH/Color Oxidation → Biological (MBBR/MBR) → Clarification → UF → RO; Brine: management plan.
  • Decision points: oxidation dose, RO recovery, brine route.
Color control Salinity strategy Compliance-ready

Sample ETP Project - Dairy

Dairy processing with high FOG/proteins and CIP sanitizers.

  • Context: FOG/protein slugs, temperature swings, sanitizer carryover.
  • Goals: Compliance, odour control, stable ops; optional energy recovery.
  • Modules (indicative): Equalization → DAF (FOG/protein capture) → Anaerobic (UASB/EGSB) or Aeration (MBBR/AS) → Clarification → UF → RO (if reuse); Sludge: Dewater.
  • Decision points: FOG control choice, anaerobic viability, CIP neutralization.
FOG control Anaerobic optional Hygiene/CIP-aware

Advisory Level & Approach

Selected pack: Feasibility & Planning (illustrative).

  • Deliverables: Options for FOG & anaerobic vs aerobic, risk register, vendor notes.
  • Outcome: Practical, clear path with staged risk reduction.

Sample ETP Project - CETP

Common Effluent Treatment Plant serving mixed member industries.

  • Context: Variable influent; shock loads; shared operating rules.
  • Goals: Compliance; fair allocation; shock-load control; reuse/ZLD readiness.
  • Modules (indicative): Large EQ → Primary (coag/floc/DAF) → Biological (MBBR/ASP/MBR) → Clarification → UF → RO/MEE (if ZLD); Brine: management policy.
  • Decision points: member pre-treatment rules, EQ capacity, shock-load protocol, ZLD economics.
Multi-member Shock-load ready Compliance control

Advisory Level & Approach

Selected pack: Implementation Alignment (illustrative).

  • Deliverables: Member pre-treatment policy, shock-load SOPs, evaluation matrix.
  • Outcome: Shared rules + vendor alignment for stable compliance.
